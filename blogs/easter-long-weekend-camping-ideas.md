The public holiday season is officially upon us! With Easter just a couple of weeks away, followed closely by Anzac Day and May Day, it is time to plan that perfect four-day escape. If you assume that iconic spots like Hinchinbrook Island are booked out six months in advance, do not panic. 

In the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to brainstorm an incredible list of camping alternatives. Whether you are looking for a quick car-camping trip near home or a rugged four-wheel-drive adventure, North Queensland has you covered.

Here are some top camping ideas for the Easter long weekend, broken down by region:

## Head North: From Paluma to Island Getaways

### Paluma Range National Park & Around
* **Paluma Dam:** A fantastic option for car camping or a short hike-in to sites near Johnston's Clearing or Shay's Clearing. It is perfect for kayaking, mountain biking into the village for a coffee, or stopping off at Birthday Creek Falls on the drive up.
* **Paradise Waterhole & Jourama Falls:** Located at the base of the range, Jourama Falls even features on-site showers for a touch of comfort.

### Wet Tropics & Beachside Escapes
* **Broadwater:** Incredibly popular with families. Pack your lilos and flotation devices to float down to the pontoon in the beautiful, flowing water.
* **Blencoe Falls:** A couple of hours drive left of Cardwell via Kennedy Range Road. Note that you will need a high-clearance 4WD, as the road can get very slippery and slidey after rain.
* **Goshen Station:** Right next door to Blencoe near Herbert River Falls. This is private property, so you must request permission to camp and be completely self-sufficient (including bringing a port-a-loo).
* **Dunk Island:** If you fancy a coastal trip, there are seven glorious waterfront campsites here complete with toilets and barbecues. You can take a 20-minute water taxi or tackle the paddle if you have a touring kayak. Just remember to pack all your own water!
* **Murray Falls & Davies Creek:** Great spots further north toward the Tablelands, though the team suggests avoiding the ultra-popular Wallaman Falls over Easter due to the massive crowds.

## Go West: Escape the Crowds
If you want to escape the Easter masses, the crew has a golden rule: the further you drive, the fewer people you will encounter. 

* **Porcupine Gorge:** A stunning option located about a six-hour drive west of Townsville. It is well worth the journey for a long weekend.
* **White Mountains National Park:** Another rugged inland option for a true getaway.
* **Undara Volcanic National Park:** Turn your long weekend into a loop and head up to check out the spectacular Undara Lava Tubes.

## Journey South: Coastal Drives and Hidden Gems

* **Salmon Creek (near Cungulla):** A fantastic beach drive-in for 4WD enthusiasts. Drive to Cungulla, follow the National Park signs to the beach, and head left (north) up the sand. You will need a National Park camping permit, but you will be rewarded with gorgeous, unique views of Cape Cleveland.
* **Cocoa Creek:** Another great local spot south of town.
* **Conway National Park:** Located down near Airlie Beach, offering a great overnighter track.
* **South Molle Island & Whitsunday Island:** Skip the resorts and book an official National Park campsite on the northern side of South Molle Island, or paddle out to Whitehaven Beach.

## What is in the Pack?
To wrap up the chat, Blair asked the guests for the one unexpected luxury they always bring along. For Cherry, it is an absolute non-negotiable: **dessert**, even on a rugged hike. For Luen, a chill long-weekend trip is best paired with **a bit of wine** every night to unwind.

Wherever you decide to pitch your tent this Easter, make sure to book your permits early, drive safely on the roads, and enjoy the great outdoors.

Happy hiking!
