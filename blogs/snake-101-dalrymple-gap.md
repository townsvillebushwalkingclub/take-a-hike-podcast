## Python Encounters and Trail Safety

In this episode of *Take A Hike*, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke from the Townsville Bushwalking Club to unpack a wild story out of Behana Gorge near Cairns. A hiker was recently ambushed by a python dangling from a tree. While pythons are non-venomous and generally keep to themselves, this unusual incident reminds us that any snake will strike if it feels cornered or threatened.

### Essential Snake Etiquette for the Trail

* **Make some noise:** Snakes can't hear ambient sounds, but they are highly sensitive to ground vibrations. Walk with heavy footfalls (without trampling the trail) to let them know you are coming.
* **Keep your distance:** If you spot a snake, back away and give it plenty of room to escape.
* **Gear up:** Wearing thick gaiters and long, loose pants creates a protective air gap against sudden strikes. 
* **Use hiking sticks:** Walking poles are excellent for feeling your way through thick brush and keeping a safe distance.
* **Hike with a buddy:** Always walk with a friend whenever possible so someone is there to help in an emergency.

## Conquering the Dalrymple Gap Track

Looking for a beautiful, historic day walk? The crew highly recommends the **Dalrymple Gap** track, located just south of Cardwell. This 10-kilometre trail follows a historic 1860s bullock cart route through the Cardwell Range. It is a fantastic option for beginners or anyone training for longer multi-day treks.

### What to Expect on the Trail

One of the absolute highlights of the hike is an iconic, historic stone bridge featuring bricks that were originally shipped all the way from Scotland. 

There are two main entry points for this walk: the Broadwater side and the Cardwell side. The *Take A Hike* team recommends starting from the **Cardwell side**. Doing so allows you to tackle the steepest hill right at the beginning of your journey, leaving you free to enjoy the gentle descent past stunning creeks and lush rainforest scenery.

### Practical Trail Tips

* **Logistics:** The trail is 10km one way and takes roughly 3.5 to 6 hours depending on your group pace. Setting up a two-car drop-off can take a while, so some hikers prefer to simply walk or run it as a 20km return trip.
* **Flora to watch out for:** Keep an eye out for "wait-a-while" vines that can snag your clothes, and steer clear of the painful stinging tree (Gympie-Gympie).
* **The post-hike reward:** Don't forget to stop in Cardwell after your walk to celebrate a great day on the trail with a famous local crab sandwich!

For more detailed maps and track information, check out **wanderstories.space** and stay safe out on the trails!
