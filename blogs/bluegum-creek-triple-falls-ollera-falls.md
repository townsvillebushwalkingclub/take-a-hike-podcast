## Hidden Treasures in Paluma and Beyond

North Queensland is packed with pristine parks and hidden trails, and on the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sat down with Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke from the Townsville Bushwalking Club to uncover some spectacular local waterfall walks.

From recent club trips to Smith Creek Falls (hidden about 10 kilometres down the PRB track from Paluma Village) to historic mining paths, the team shared options for every fitness level. Whether you are chasing an easy weekend stroll with the family or a grueling, off-track wilderness adventure, we have you covered.

---

## The Easy (to Medium) Option: Triple Falls & The Bluegum Creek Loop

If you want a stunning walk that offers a bit of flexibility, Cherry Judge recommends heading up to Paluma to tackle the Bluegum Creek area. 

### Chasing Triple Falls
For an easy there-and-back walk, your target destination is **Triple Falls**. It is a gorgeous swimming hole that is spectacular at any time of year, making it a perfect destination for an October trek.

*   **Finding the Trail:** Head to Paluma and look for the official National Park **H Track**. About 500 to 600 meters along the H Track, you will spot a National Park sign. 
*   **The Secret Entrance:** The real trick is finding the turnoff! Right by the sign, you essentially step over it into a "parting curtain of vines." The first two meters are hidden to keep casual strollers out, but once you step through, the trail is well-defined and clearly marked with Paluma markers.
*   **The Descent:** The walk down is beautiful, but keep an eye out for the final 10 meters. There is a small rope descent right to the bottom of the falls. If you are hiking with young children, they will definitely need some assistance here (and you might hear a tiny bit of whinging on the climb back up!).

### Extending to the Bluegum Creek Loop
If you want a more substantial historic walk, do not turn back at the waterfall. Return to the main junction, turn right, and continue along the **Bluegum Creek Trail**.

This 11-kilometre loop is peppered with creeks and waterfalls, including about three to four straightforward creek crossings. Just 300 meters downstream from the junction, you will hit a massive, long lagoon perfect for a cool swim. 

As you climb back up the other side of the gully, you will pass **Miners Dump Creek**, **Mine Creek Falls**, and the historic **Hang Flat Mine**. This area was once an active tungsten mine, and the trail is scattered with old water races and historical artifacts like vintage bottles and equipment. 

> **Safety Note:** There are several old mining shafts near the Hang Flat Mine site that do not have safety railings. Keep a close watch on your step, leave the historical artifacts exactly where you find them, and be prepared for a steep, sweaty climb out of the gully!

## The Hard (to Extreme) Option: Ollera Creek Falls & Beyond

Looking for something that will truly test your limits? Luen Warneke brought an extreme challenge to the table: **Ollera Creek Falls**. 

Located just before the Paluma turnoff, you have likely driven right over the Ollera Creek Bridge without realizing the rugged paradise hiding upstream. 

### Destination: Lizard Gorge
For a demanding but rewarding day hike, you can trek from the lower causeway up to **Lizard Gorge**. 
*   **The Route:** The rough track follows alongside the creek, heads up a spur, and drops back down into rugged terrain. 
*   **The Reward:** Lizard Gorge rewards hikers with stunning, emerald-blue waters. 
*   **The Distance:** A return trip to the gorge is roughly 10 kilometres, taking about 4.5 to 5 hours at an average walking pace.

### Pushing on to Ollera Falls
If you push past Lizard Gorge toward Ollera Creek Falls, the difficulty spikes massively. The trail vanishes, and you will face intense, rugged terrain and extensive rock hopping along the creek bed.

While it is only about 16 kilometres return, the technical nature of the rock hopping means this hike can easily take **8 to 12 hours return**-and if it is drizzly or wet, the rocks become incredibly hazardous. 

### The Epic Epilogue: Ollera to Paluma Village
For the truly extreme adventurers, some hikers continue past Ollera Falls, pushing straight through the dense rainforest to connect with the old **PRB track** and the Smith Creek Falls track, eventually topping out at Paluma Village. 

There is no rock climbing or rope work required, but it involves navigating steep, trackless rainforest. Local volunteers are slowly working to recut the historic PRB track and its side shoots-which were heavily decimated by Cyclone Yasi-with the hopes of making these beautiful falls easily accessible from the village in the future.

## Practical Tips Before You Hike

*   **Access:** The typical entry point via Caleo Road ends at private property. While there are alternative routes to enter the creek, it is always best to seek permission from landowners if you are crossing private boundaries.
*   **Navigation:** These trails are not currently on Qtopo. However, you can find detailed information, maps, and GPX files on the Wanderstories website.
*   **Hike with the Club:** Want to explore these trails with an experienced crew who knows the tracks inside out? Reach out to us directly or email the club via our website at **townsvillebushwalkingclub.com**.

Stay safe on the trails, watch your step around those old mining gullies, and happy hiking!
