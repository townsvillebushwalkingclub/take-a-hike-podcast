After three long years of closure following the 2019 floods, one of Townsville's absolute favourite hiking destinations has officially reopened. That is right, the full **Alligator Creek Falls** trail is back and open for business!

In the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Luen Warneke and Cherry Judge to break down everything you need to know before tackling this iconic day hike.

---

## The Main Event: Alligator Creek Falls Trail

Located in Bowling Green National Park, just a short drive south of Townsville past Alligator Creek/Vandale, this trail is a staple for local adventurers. But make no mistake: if you are planning to go all the way to the falls, it is a full day out.

*   **Distance:** 17 km return from the main car park.
*   **Time:** Around 5 hours at an average walking pace (3 km/h), including breaks.
*   **Terrain:** The first section follows an exposed, sunny firebreak road. It can get very hot, so picking a cooler day or walking just after the rainy season is highly recommended. Once you cross Cockatoo Creek, the trail transitions into shaded, beautiful rainforest.

### Surviving the Creek Crossings
There are four or five creek crossings along the route. While they are a massive highlight and offer a great chance to cool down, they can be challenging after heavy rain. The very first crossing can reach up to chest deep, so always check the conditions, prepare to get wet, and exercise caution.

## Two Wheels or Two Feet: The Alternative Route

Did you know you can also access the falls on a mountain bike? If you cross the river early as you enter the national park, you can ride up and over the firebreak roads. 

Known locally as "Elliot's Revenge" (the old Telstra Tower Track), this steep but rewarding riding trail is fully mapped on the **Trail Forks** app. It eventually connects back with the regular single-track walking route, giving you a completely different way to experience the park.

---

## The True Gem: The Infinity Pools at the Top

For most people, reaching the base of the spectacular Alligator Creek Falls is reward enough for a long hike. But for the adventurous, there is an unofficial, flagged track winding up the right-hand side of the falls.

> **Safety Warning:** Do not attempt to scramble straight up the slippery, rocky face of the falls. The consequences of a slip are incredibly high, and emergency services have performed many rescues here. 

Instead, look for the pink, orange, or blue surveyor tape navigating safely through the trees. It is a steep climb up, but it rewards you with breathtaking views across Saddle Mountain, stunning upper waterfalls, and pristine, crystal-clear infinity pools.

--- 

## Discover a Secret Spot Just Up From the Carpark

Not up for a grueling 17 km trek? You do not have to miss out on the magic. The car park area has great swimming holes perfect for a family picnic while others head out for the long hike. 

But if you want to escape the crowds, Luen shared an insider secret: **The Gorge**.

Located just a few hundred metres upstream from the main car park waterhole, this hidden gem is rarely visited. You can rock-hop directly upstream from the car park, or walk towards Cockatoo Creek and cut across to follow the water downstream. It is a beautiful, private swimming spot that rivals Crystal Creek, without the massive crowds.

Whether you are looking for a relaxing dip near the car park, a challenging mountain bike loop, or a full-day hike to the top of the falls, Alligator Creek is ready to welcome you back. 

Happy hiking!
