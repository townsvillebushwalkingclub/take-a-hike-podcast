## Island Adventures on Maggie

With the North Queensland weather finally starting to cool down a fraction, the "Take A Hike" crew is back to chat about one of Townsville's absolute favourite outdoor playgrounds: **Magnetic Island**. 

Host **Blair Woodcock** sits down with **Luen Warneke** and **Cherry Judge** from the Townsville Bushwalking Club to unpack the best trails the island has to offer. Whether you are looking for an easy stroll straight to the pub or a strenuous, off-track navigational challenge, Maggie Island has something for every level of hiker.

---

## The Best Trails on Magnetic Island

### Hawkings Point
If you are after a short walk with a massive payoff, Luen recommends **Hawkings Point**. Located at the end of Picnic Bay, this 1.2km straight trail leads you up to a stunning granite boulder.
* **The View:** Overlooks both Picnic Bay and Rocky Bay.
* **Top Tip:** It is an incredibly Instagrammable location and the perfect spot for a North Queensland sunset.
* **Bonus Adventure:** There is an unofficial, rough goat track off the main trail that scrambles down into Rocky Bay for those who want to explore further.

### Nellie Bay to Arcadia (via The Sphinx)
Cherry's personal favourite starts at the end of Mandalay Avenue in Nellie Bay. The track climbs up and along the ridge, offering stunning winter breezes. 
* **The Junction:** Once you reach the top, you can head down toward Arcadia or continue toward the Forts.
* **Seasonal Waterfalls:** If you hike this track just after the rains, take the Arcadia route to see the seasonal waterfalls and the impressive **Sphinx lookout**, a natural rock formation that looks just like its namesake.

### The Forts to Florence Bay Steps
For those heading out to the historic Forts, **National Parks** has recently installed brand-new steps at the end of the track. These steps follow an old World War II road that was previously washed out and off-limits, leading walkers safely all the way down into **Florence Bay**. It is a fantastic way to experience the five iconic bays: Florence, Radical, Balding, Arthur, and Horseshoe.

## Hidden Gems and Off-Track Challenges

If you want to escape the crowds and dive into some of the island's military history or rugged peaks, the team shared a few lesser-known spots:

* **Searchlight Tower Track:** An easy, often overlooked lookout track located between Arthur and Florence Bay. Just follow the road down from the Forts car park to find the old WWII concrete structure.
* **Dragon's Back:** Visible from Horseshoe Bay looking out to sea on the right, this ridge looks like a sleeping dragon. Locals often kayak over to the bay and hike up the ridge.
* **Mount Paluma & Mount Cook:** Magnetic Island features two official summits. While Mount Cook is the highest, **Mount Paluma** sits to the right of the Mandalay Avenue track. It features historic, overgrown stone stairs and retaining walls from an old trail, offering beautiful views across to Castle Hill and Nellie Bay. 

> **Safety Note:** Trails like Mount Cook and Mount Paluma are fully overgrown and off-track. You should only attempt these if you have solid navigation skills and a reliable map. Do not rely on pink tape or trail markers!
