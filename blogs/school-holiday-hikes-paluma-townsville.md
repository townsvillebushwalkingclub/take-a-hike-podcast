## Tiring Out the Kids in Nature's Playground

School holidays are officially here! If you are looking for ways to entertain the family, burn off some extra energy, or just escape the coastal heat for a day, the latest episode of the **Take a Hike** podcast has you covered. 

Host Blair Woodcock sat down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars **Cherry Judge** and **Luen Warneke** to map out the best local trails, swimming holes, and hidden gems. From toddler-friendly lookouts to epic full-day waterfall expeditions, there is something in the Paluma range for every age and energy level.

---

## Quick and Easy Walks (Perfect for Little Legs)

If you have toddlers or kids who aren't quite ready for a mountain trek, Paluma offers spectacular rewards with very minimal walking.

*   **McClellan's Lookout:** Literally a 50-meter stroll from the village car park. It is the perfect first stop to get a view of the region.
*   **Witt's Lookout:** Continue past McClellan's for a short walk to the upper and lower lookouts. You'll get stunning views across Halifax Bay and Crystal Creek, complete with picnic tables for a morning snack.
*   **Birthday Creek Falls:** Located just on your way out from the dam, this is a quick 200-meter walk down to a series of beautiful cascading falls. It features shallow dipping pools that are absolutely perfect for younger children to safely splash around in.

## Mid-Length Day Walks and Swimming Holes

Ready to get your boots a little dirtier? These trails require a bit more effort but deliver massive rewards.

*   **Cloudy Creek to Ethel Creek Falls:** Starting from the village, you can head down to Cloudy Creek for a quick dip, then cross over and hike about an hour and a half to Ethel Creek Falls. The waterfall features a dramatic 10-meter drop into a large, deep swimming pool. It features a few ups and downs, making it perfect for kids who don't mind a bit of a walk.
*   **The Rock Gardens:** Located just a couple of kilometers from Paluma Dam along the track to Crystal Creek Falls. Taking the little detour into the Rock Gardens feels like stepping straight into *Jurassic Park*.
*   **Jourama Falls:** Situated slightly further down the range, Jourama Falls is an easy walk that leads to tiered, step-like swimming holes. Because the pools are layered, it is easy for families to spread out and find their own private oasis.

> **Local Tip:** If you are heading up into the rainforest trails, keep an eye out for local orchids. September is orchid season, and the variety of blooms is stunning. And don't worry too much about those famous epic Paluma leeches... they are usually only active after heavy rain!

## The Next Level: Paluma Ridge Diorite Falls & DCK Shelter

For families with teenagers or seasoned hikers looking for a true bush adventure, Luen and Cherry highlighted the **Paluma Ridge Diorite Falls**. 

This is a challenging, undulating 15 to 22-kilometer loop that starts at the back of Paluma Dam. The destination features a massive, broad slab of diorite rock where water cascades down into a gorgeous swimming pool. Because the track is maintained entirely by volunteers, you may have to navigate the occasional fallen tree.

If you want to turn it into an overnight adventure, you can book the **DCK Shelter**. It is a fantastic, well-maintained hut on an old tin mining site. The surrounding area still holds historical artifacts like old pickaxes, rusty batteries, and bottle dumps.

> **Important:** If you visit DCK Shelter, remember the bushwalking golden rule: appreciate the history, look at the artifacts, but **do not touch or take anything**.

## Planning Your Trip

Before you pack the car and head up the mountain, preparation is key. Make sure to download your maps ahead of time, as reliable phone reception can be scarce in the dense rainforest. 

Two incredible resources for local trail maps, compass bearings, and marker disc guides are:
*   **paluma.org** (Essential for downloading maps and getting permission for the DCK Shelter)
*   **wanderstories.space** (Excellent for comprehensive local trail maps)

Once you finish your hike, do not forget to stop by the Paluma Village for a fresh scone and a coffee. The Rainforest Inn is open on Sundays and is the perfect way to wrap up a day in the cooler mountain air.

*Stay safe on the trails, and happy hiking!*
