## Epic Drops, Surf and Turf, and Life with Luen

Welcome back to the Take A Hike blog! In our latest episode, hosts Blair Woodcock and Cherry Judge sat down with regular guest Luen Warneke (from Wanderstories) and a very special guest: marine biologist Professor Jody Rummer. 

As it turns out, Jody is also Luen's partner of five years. Together, they form the ultimate 'surf and turf' adventuring duo. The crew covered everything from what makes a waterfall truly spectacular to the hilarious survival testing grounds of couples who hike together.

---

## What Makes the Ultimate Waterfall?

Before diving into the couples' chat, Blair grilled Luen on a tough question: *What makes the perfect waterfall?*

According to Luen, a great waterfall isn't just about a massive drop (though Wallaman Falls is undeniably jaw-dropping). It is about the **journey** and the unique features, like hidden caves or rugged trails. If a waterfall is only five meters from the car park, it takes away from the experience. You need that hike to get the endorphins flowing!

### Luen's Top Waterfall Picks:
* **Moochoopa Falls:** Flowing off Mount Bartle Frere (Queensland's highest peak) in the Russell River area. It features a rough 15-kilometer bush track, making the reward well worth the effort.
* **The Terraces:** A fantastic spot featuring multiple cascading waterfalls, all within a 30-minute drive of Townsville.
* **Global Favorites:** For international scale, Luen points to Iguazu Falls and the massive waterfalls on the North Vietnam and China border.

## Surviving "Life with Luen": Couples Who Adventure Together

Jody joined the studio to share the reality of sharing a life-and a trail map-with an extreme adventurer. Their relationship was built on high adrenaline from day one. For their first date, Luen took Jody canyoning and abseiling down **Jourama Falls**. For the second date, Jody raised the stakes by taking Luen on a night climb up the face of **Castle Hill**, tackling the three-pitch multi-pitch route known as *Vision*.

While Jody lives by the rule of **'high effort, high reward'** (give her a stunning summit view or a beautiful waterfall any day), she admits that Luen's plans can occasionally be a bit grandiose. It has even led to her coining a famous catchphrase on Saddle Mountain: *"This is not enjoyable, Luen."*

> "Couples that adventure together, stay together. But there have been a few times we nearly murdered each other!" 
> - Professor Jody Rummer

### The Infamous Peter Bot De-Leeching Incident
One of the funniest highlights of the episode involved a grueling trek in the Mount Sorrow area at Peter Bot. After being swarmed by hundreds of leeches, the pair finally reached a rocky outcrop where they could strip down to get rid of the pests. 

Moments later, Jody took a tumble down a razorback ridgeline. Hanging upside down from her backpack, she called out for help-only for a completely naked, barefoot Luen to come charging through the rainforest to rescue her. It is the ultimate testament to bonding in the wild!

## From Rainforests to Reefs: Local Hotspots

Townsville is incredibly blessed with diverse terrain. Jody and Luen highlighted some of their favorite local ecosystems to explore:

* **The Zen of the Rainforest:** Jody loves the lush ferns, beautiful trees, and fungi. Just watch your navigation-she admits to once getting so turned around photographing mushrooms that she started hiking the complete opposite way!
* **Mount Stuart:** A great local classic for a quick weekend morning workout.
* **Mount Storth (Bowling Green Bay National Park):** A steep, dry, straight-up hike filled with grass plants, offering absolutely gorgeous coastal views just 30 to 40 minutes from town.

### Beyond the Dirt: French Polynesia Drift Dives
As a marine biologist, Jody also brings Luen into her world. She recalled an incredible 15 to 20-meter drift dive in French Polynesia where they meditated while drifting with the current, surrounded by blacktip reef sharks, tiger sharks, and rays.
