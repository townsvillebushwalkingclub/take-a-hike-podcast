## Leaving No Trace: The Truth About Trail Hygiene

Before diving back into the world of extreme adventure racing, hosts Blair Woodcock, Luen Warneke, and Cherry Judge kicked off this week's episode with a topic every bushwalker thinks about but rarely discusses openly: trail hygiene. 

When you are out in the remote wilderness around North Queensland, how do you keep clean without harming the environment? Luen and Blair shared their go-to methods and top tips for staying fresh on the trail:

*   **Hand Sanitizer Gels:** A trail staple for cleaning hands before meals. As a bonus, Luen points out that because of the high alcohol content, it doubles as an excellent emergency fire starter.
*   **Biodegradable All-Purpose Wash:** Products like Sea to Summit's multi-purpose liquid soap are great for dishes and clothes, but remember the golden rule: always wash and empty your gray water 50 to 100 meters away from natural streams to protect local aquatic life.
*   **Eco-Friendly Wipes:** If you use wet wipes, ensure they are 100% biodegradable Bambaroo (like Bambaroo). Standard wet wipes contain plastic and do not break down. No matter what, always pack them out. Luen recalled seeing the western summit of Mount Bartle Frere littered with discarded wipes-let's keep our summits pristine! 
*   **Pre-packaged Napkins:** A clever tip from the studio is saving unused paper napkins from restaurants to reuse as trail toilet paper.

## Local Legends and Remote Rainforest Encounters

The team welcomed back local adventure cyclist and racer Mitch Nissan for part two of his interview. The connection between Mitch and Luen goes way back to a chance encounter in Far North Queensland. Luen recalled hiking deep into a trackless, remote rainforest for two days, only to summit **Mount Pieter Botte** and find Mitch and his mates casually emerging from the bush carrying lilos, ready to float down **Roaring Meg Creek**.

They also reminisced about a grueling training session for the God's Zone race. Starting from Townsville at 1:00 AM, the crew drove north to Josephine Falls, hiked up and over **Mount Bartle Frere** (Queensland's highest peak), plunged off-track through dense rainforest to the Russell River, and paddled out to Golden Hole. The cost of admission? Hundreds of leeches, including one that famously found its way into Mitch's eye!

### Mitch's Top Local Spots
Right now, Mitch is spending a lot of time sharpening his navigation skills in the **Clement State Forest**. Located on the border of Paluma National Park, it is widely known as the start of the trail up to **Godwin's Peak**, boasting incredible waterways, waterfalls, and endless space to explore.

## What's Next? From Tinaroo to the Swedish Archipelago

Mitch isn't slowing down anytime soon. Locally, he is helping coordinate the **Grin and Bear It** multi-sport race at **Lake Tinaroo** on July 8th. This unique event features swimming, paddling, mountain biking, and running. To cater to those who aren't fans of swimming, they have introduced a paddle-only alternative leg this year.

After that, Mitch is heading international to tackle the **One Water Race** outside Stockholm, Sweden this August. This brutal 250km archipelago navigation race consists of 50km of ocean swimming and 200km of running across islands.

To close out the chat, Blair hit Mitch with the ultimate rapid-fire question: If you could only do one for the rest of your life-trek, run, or paddle-what stays? Mitch's pick? **Paddling** (mostly to save his knees!).
