## Leaving No Trace (and What to Do When Nature Calls)

Let's be honest, if you spend enough time out on the trails or embarking on an overnight adventure, you are eventually going to run into a situation where you need to do a "number two" in the bush. While Tassie might be famous for spectacular porcelain drop toilets with stunning open-air mountain and rainforest views, remote hiking usually means making your own arrangements. 

When there are no facilities available, practicing proper hygiene etiquette is vital to keep our beautiful North Queensland parks pristine. 

### The Rules of the Trail

*   **The 200-Metre Rule:** In Queensland, National Parks regulations require you to find a spot at least 200 metres away from any water body, trail, or campsite.
*   **Dig Deep:** Use a trowel, a stick, or the heel of your boot to dig a hole at least 15 centimetres deep. This ensures animals won't dig it back up.
*   **Pack it Out:** While some hikers rely on leaves or choose to bury their paper, the best practice is to pack out your toilet paper. Alternatively, modern solutions like portable travel bidets are picking up steam as a highly effective, hygienic option.

## The Lower Big Crystal Creek Circuit

If you are looking for a fantastic half-day adventure that is perfect for the family, this low-elevation walk at the base of the Paluma Range is a hidden gem. It offers plenty of opportunities to cool off without the grueling climbs of our usual massive day trails.

### Route at a Glance

*   **Start Point:** Mango Tree Car Park (the Rockslides Car Park) on Spielberg Hill Road, right where you cross Nolan's Gully.
*   **The Track:** From the car park, walk along the road past the Rockslides sidetrack until you reach the causeway at the junction of Cloudy Creek and Ethel Creek.
*   **The Hidden Gem:** Head downstream from the causeway about 100 metres to discover a beautiful, lesser-known waterfall that many visitors miss.
*   **The Return:** Continue down into the junction of Big Crystal Creek, navigating the big boulders as the creek transitions into a smaller, sandy section that loops back toward the car.

> **Tip from the Club:** This area features open bushland rather than thick rainforest canopy, so make sure to pack a hat, sun protective gear, sunscreen, your swimmers, and always carry a map or a navigation app like AllTrails.
