The Easter long weekend is finally here! If you cancelled your camping plans because of the intense Townsville humidity, or missed out on booking accommodation before everything filled up, we have the perfect solution. On the latest episode of the Take A Hike podcast, host Blair Woodcock sat down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to share some fantastic, easy one-day adventures up in Paluma.

Paluma is only about an hour drive from Townsville, and it is always a few degrees cooler up on the range. It is the perfect place to escape the heat, get the kids away from their screens, and walk off all that Easter chocolate. Here are a few great options discussed in this episode.

### Ethel Creek Falls
Cherry Judge recently took a trip up to Ethel Creek Falls and highly recommends it as a family-friendly option. It is a lovely four-hour return hike, but it only takes that long because you will want to take your time and enjoy the beautiful scenery. 

The track is well-worn and completely marked. To get there, park at the Witts Lookout car park as you enter Paluma Village, head towards Cloudy Creek, and follow the pink markers and the little rope across the creek. The water is cool and refreshing, making it an excellent spot for a swim. 

*Practical Tip:* The mosquitoes are pretty vicious up there at the moment, so make sure you pack plenty of insect repellent!

### Birthday Creek to Little Birthday Creek
If you want an introduction to off-track creek walking, Cherry and the Bushwalking Club recently explored this stunning route. Start by parking at the Birthday Creek Falls car park along the Dam Road. It is an easy 10-minute walk (about 400 meters) down the official National Park track to the falls.

From there, the real adventure begins. You jump right into the creek and follow it down until it connects with Little Birthday Creek, which eventually spits you back out onto the Dam Road. It features stunning aqua-blue water, lush fern-lined banks, and majestic canopy views. This section takes about two to three hours and covers just a few kilometers.

*Practical Tip:* Because creek beds are uneven and rocky, wear a pair of old hiking shoes that you do not mind getting wet rather than flimsy water shoes. You will also need a map and basic navigation skills, as there are multiple tributaries.

### The Rock Gardens and Beyond
If you want to extend your day near the dam, you can take a shortcut from the creek up to the Rock Gardens. It is a magical, beginner-friendly spot filled with massive mossy boulders and ferns. 

For those looking for a longer day walk, you can connect the Rock Gardens to Wilfred's Lookout, and then head out to Crystal Creek. It is about an 11-kilometer loop, but it is all on-trail from the dam and makes for a wonderful day out.

### Witts Lookout
Blair highlighted Witts Lookout as a classic option that belongs on everyone's list. Starting from the McClellan's Lookout area, you follow the same track that goes to Cloudy Creek, but take the turn-off to Witts Lookout. It features both an upper and a lower lookout, offering unbeatable views right down the valley.

### Mossy Falls (The Ultimate Micro-Adventure)
Luen Warneke teased a "micro-hike" that is anywhere from 5 to 20 meters long. Located on the right-hand side of the Dam Road just before you reach Lake Paluma, Mossy Falls is a favorite spot for local photographers. It is a beautiful, mossy granite slide where water cascades down. Find a safe spot to pull off the road and take a quick look.

### More Resources
If you need detailed directions, trail maps, or coordinates for these locations, check out paluma.org or search for them on wanderstories.space, where you can find full articles and trail guides.

No matter which trail you choose this weekend, please stay safe. It is still hot out there, so pack plenty of water, tell someone where you are going, and enjoy the beautiful cool rainforest. Happy hiking!
