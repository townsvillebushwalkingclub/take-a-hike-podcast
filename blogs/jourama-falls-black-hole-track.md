As the North Queensland weather begins its shift into the warmer months, finding the perfect swimming spot becomes top priority. On the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sat down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to chat about one of the region's absolute jewels: Jourama Falls.

Located just a comfortable 40-minute drive north of Townsville, Jourama Falls offers everything from a relaxed family day trip to an intense, heart-pumping vertical scramble. 

Here are the three ways you can experience Jourama Falls and the Black Hole Track, ranging from easiest to most adventurous.

---

## Option 1: The Family Picnic and Lookout (Easy)

If you are looking for an accessible day out, the main day-use area at Jourama Falls is tough to beat. Thanks to recent road upgrades, getting there is smoother than ever. 

* **The Terraces:** Just 100 meters into the walk, you can turn right into Waterview Creek or continue across the timber bridge to reach the lower rock terraces. These natural tiered structures create dozens of mini rock pools, meaning you can easily find your own private swimming hole for the day.
* **The Lookout:** If you want a view without the sweat, follow the main trail up to the lookout. It is a well-marked track under 500 meters long, fitted with non-slip plastic sheeting on the ground, making it an easy stroll for all fitness levels.

Want to stay longer? National Parks maintains a beautiful campground on-site complete with showers. Just remember that there is **no internet access** at Jourama, so you must book your camping permit online before you leave home.

## Option 2: The Vertical Scramble to the Top (Moderate to Hard)

For those who want a bit more of a challenge, the trail doesn't end at the lookout. From there, you can transition into an off-track scramble that follows the spur up to the cliff edge.

* **The Terrain:** This is a steep, almost vertical 40 to 45-minute slog over friable, dry dirt. Expect to take about an hour to an hour and a half from the car park to reach the top.
* **What to Bring:** Good, grippy hiking shoes are non-negotiable. Luen also recommends bringing a pair of gloves, as you will find yourself grabbing onto tree roots and branches to pull yourself up.
* **The Reward:** Once you contour around the cliff edge, you will drop onto a stunning granite slab right at the very top of Jourama Falls, offering incredible valley views and an unforgettable spot for a break.

## Option 3: The Famous Black Hole Track (Experienced Adventurers)

If you cross the creek at the top of Jourama Falls and keep climbing, the terrain gets even steeper as you enter dense rainforest. This is the gateway to the legendary **Black Hole**.

Fun fact: Luen Warneke actually coined the name "Black Hole" as a teenager after spotting the deep, 25-meter-deep rock fracture as a prominent black dot on Google Maps!

### Surviving the Rainforest
Once you cross into the rainforest zone, vigilance is key. You will need to keep a sharp eye out for local hazards like Wait-A-While and Gimpy Gimpy (the stinging tree). Wearing full-sleeve clothing and long pants is highly recommended to protect your skin.

To get there, hike up steeply from the creek crossing until you hit a circular junction marker, then take a right turn down into the canyon. You will be greeted by a deep, dark fracture where crystal-clear water cascades down the rocks into a spectacular swimming hole. For the return journey, experienced hikers can rock-hop downstream through a series of smaller waterfalls back to the top of Jourama Falls.

### Can you hike all the way to Paluma?
Yes! For the ultimate endurance test, you can follow the track from the top of Jourama all the way through to Paluma Dam or Paluma Village (passing Hermit Creek Falls along the way). Depending on your pace, this demanding trek will take anywhere from 4 to 10 hours.

## Practicing 'Leave No Trace' at Jourama

Because Jourama Falls is such a popular destination, the team emphasized the importance of keeping our footprints minimal. 

### Avoid Trail Braiding
Because the upper sections to the Black Hole are unmarked, hikers often venture off-track to avoid mud or fallen logs. This causes "trail braiding," which widens the path, destroys vegetation, and erodes the environment. Stick to the main established trail. If you aren't sure of the way, reach out to the Townsville Bushwalking Club for maps and guidance rather than cutting a new path.

### Fireplace Etiquette
While there are official fire pits at the designated national park campground, you **must bring your own firewood**. Foraging for wood or chopping down branches inside the National Park is strictly illegal. If you don't bring your own wood, skip the fire entirely.
