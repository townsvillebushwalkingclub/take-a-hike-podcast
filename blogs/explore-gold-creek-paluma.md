## Heading Back Up the Range to Paluma

It is time to head back up the range for another creek exploration in Paluma National Park! In the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to dive into the lush, remote, and fascinating world of **Gold Creek**.

Paluma is packed with hidden gems, but Gold Creek offers something truly unique for off-track adventurers. 

---

## The Mystery of the Water Divergence

If you love unique geographical features, Gold Creek has a rare treat. While most creeks feature smaller tributaries and forks that flow into a single main stream, Luen highlights an unusual **water divergence** hidden in the upper reaches.

At a specific, small saddle high up the system, Gold Creek can actually split during periods of high flow, spilling over and running down into **Cairn Creek**. While Cairn Creek generally runs a bit drier, seeing this rare forking action in action after heavy rain is a spectacular sight for any bushwalking enthusiast.

## Off-Track Adventures vs. The Easier Route

The crew reminisced about a remote trip a couple of years back. Starting at Paluma Dam and maintaining elevation to **Foxlee's Lookout**, they headed entirely off-track, descending down the watershed directly into the headwaters of Gold Creek. 

> "Everything turns mossy green and, oh, it's so pretty. Some small cascades and lovely pools to swim in along the way."

If you are following the creek all the way down, you will eventually hit the stunning **Gold Creek Falls**. 

### How to get to Gold Creek Falls:
* **The On-Track Route:** The most straightforward way to see the falls is via the **Bullocky Toms track** (which is now an official, marked track). Heading upwards, Gold Creek Falls is the first major waterfall you will encounter.
* **The Off-Track Route:** For the highly experienced, starting at Paluma Dam, heading to Foxlee's Lookout, and navigating the remote upper watershed reveals the trickling headwaters before they plunge over the falls.

## Transitioning to Remote Bushwalking Safely

Let's be honest: there is a massive difference between a weekend stroll up Mount Louisa and navigating the thick rainforest, wait-a-while vines, and trackless creeks of Paluma. If you want to step up your hiking game and explore these remote pockets, here is the advice from Blair, Cherry, and Luen:

* **Don't Go Alone:** Always head out with someone more experienced, a trusted friend, or join a structured group like the Townsville Bushwalking Club.
* **Master Navigation:** Having core navigation knowledge is critical. It is incredibly easy to get lost in the moment when following beautiful creek lines.
* **Use GPS and Waypoints:** Keep a reliable digital map on hand. Tools like AllTrails allow you to drop waypoints at known track junctions before you explore. 
* **Take Safe Explores:** Start on a marked trail, like the walk to Foxlee's Lookout. When you cross a creek, do a small, safe explore just around the corner to build your confidence while keeping a strict eye on your map.
