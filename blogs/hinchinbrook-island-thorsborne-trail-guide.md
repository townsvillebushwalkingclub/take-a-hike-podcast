If you ask any bushwalker in North Queensland to name the ultimate local bucket-list adventure, one destination always comes out on top: **Hinchinbrook Island**. Often described as a real-life Jurassic Park, this prehistoric-looking national park island is home to the Thorsborne Trail, widely regarded as one of the world's best multi-day hikes. 

In the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to chat about all things Hinchinbrook. Whether you want to stick to the managed track or tackle the rugged off-track summits, here is the group's collective wisdom on conquering this iconic island.

---

## The Thorsborne Trail: Fast or Slow?

The Thorsborne Trail itself stretches roughly **32 kilometres** along the island's eastern coast. Because it is relatively short, it attracts a massive variety of hikers and athletes.

* **The Ultra-Runners:** Hardcore fitness enthusiasts have been known to kayak over from Lucinda, run the entire 32km trail in a single day, and kayak right back. 
* **The Traditionalists:** Most hikers take the recommended **four days and three nights** to truly absorb the landscape.
* **The Relaxers:** Cherry actually recommends extending the trip to **five days**. Taking an extra "zero day" at Zoe Bay gives you an entire day to relax and swim without having to pack up your gear and hike.

### Which Direction is Best?
While the traditional route runs from north to south (starting at Ramsay Bay and finishing at George Point), Blair and his friends opted for the reverse, walking south to north. Both ways offer incredible scenery, but starting from the north is the most common choice for club members.

## On-Trail Highlights

Every square inch of Hinchinbrook is beautiful, but the team highlighted a few absolute must-see spots along the main trail:

* **Nina's Peak:** An absolute must-do detour. You can drop your heavy packs at the saddle of the main track and make the relatively easy climb up with just a water bottle. The top offers spectacular views looking out toward The Thumb and Mount Bowen. 
* **Little Ramsay Bay:** Cherry notes that sitting on this massive beach having dinner under a full moon is an unforgettable experience.
* **Zoe Bay:** A crowd favourite. Beyond the main campground, look out for **Cypress Pine Creek** (or North Zoe Creek) just to the north. A short, couple-hundred-meter walk up the creek reveals stunning, crystal-clear blue waterholes and gorges that have become famous on social media.
* **Mulligan Falls:** The only inland campsite on the route. It features a beautiful waterfall populated by incredibly inquisitive fish. 

> **Historical Trivia:** The early European explorers who mapped the island had a unique sense of naming conventions. They named many of the prominent mountain features after body parts, giving us names like the Wart, the Fingers, and the Thumb!

## Pushing Limits: Off-Track Summits

For experienced navigators looking for a challenge beyond the Thorsborne Trail, Hinchinbrook's interior contains rugged, off-track peaks. Luen Warneke has spent plenty of time exploring these remote areas:

* **Mount Bowen:** The highest mountain on the island and the most popular summit challenge.
* **Mount Diamantina:** Luen's personal highlight. It features an iconic, massive square boulder on the summit (visible from the highway on the mainland) and offers an extra technical challenge to climb.
* **Mount Straloch:** Home to the historic B-24 Liberator bomber wreck. Note that you cannot access Straloch from the Thorsborne Trail; it requires an entirely separate off-track navigation route.

## Important Safety & Conservation Tips

### Crocodile Safety
Yes, Hinchinbrook is croc country. While you are unlikely to spot them on the high parts of the walking trail itself, they are absolutely present in the mangroves and waterways. Cherry and Luen both note that **Zoe Bay** is a known hotspot, where you can regularly see croc slides near the boat landing area and eyes reflecting in the water at night. Stay vigilant, camp in designated areas, and stay away from the water's edge at night.

### Strictly No Campfires
One of the biggest bad habits seen on the island is people lighting campfires. 

> "Fires are not allowed anywhere on that island," warns Blair. 

Scavenging for firewood decimates the pristine bush, and a runaway wildfire would be catastrophic for this untouched ecosystem. Do not follow the bad examples on social media-leave the campfire out, or expect a call from a Queensland Parks and Wildlife Service (QPWS) compliance officer.

---

## How to Score an Elusive Permit

To preserve the wilderness experience, QPWS limits the island to just **40 people at any one time**. This makes booking a permit incredibly competitive. 

* **Book 6 months in advance:** This is the standard window for national park bookings.
* **The Midnight Refresh:** Permits operate on rolling dates that update at midnight every night. If you miss out, keep refreshing the system late at night to catch cancellations.
* At just $6.50 per person, per night, it is an incredibly affordable world-class adventure if you can secure a spot.

For official maps, track conditions, and to book your permits, head directly to the **Queensland Parks and Wildlife Services** website. 

Until next time, stay safe on the trails and happy hiking!
