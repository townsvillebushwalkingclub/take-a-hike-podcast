## Discovering a Hidden Paradise on Mt Elliott

If you ask long-term North Queensland hikers to name their absolute favourite trail around Townsville, there is a very high chance they will point you toward the southern edge of Bowling Green Bay National Park. Nestled on the slopes of Mt Elliott lies an incredible network of creeks boasting waterfall after waterfall, pristine cascades, and natural rock terraces that look almost too perfect to be real. 

In this episode of *Take a Hike*, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke from the Townsville Bushwalking Club to chat about this pristine hiking wonderland and explain how you can experience it for yourself.

---

## The Allure of the Terraces

Discovered by the Townsville Bushwalking Club way back in the 1960s, the Terraces have remained a crown jewel of local hiking for over 60 years. Visually, it is a completely unique landscape. Imagine massive, tiered rock formations with crystal-clear water flowing over them, creating a staircase of spectacular slides and pools. 

While popular spots like the Palm Creek Rock Slides have seen access routes close over the years due to the closure of the nearby caravan park, the upper creek sections remain a high-reward destination for those willing to make the journey.

### Grading the Trails
If you are exploring this side of Mt Elliott, the creek systems offer a variety of options depending on how much effort you want to put in:

*   **The Terraces:** High reward for medium effort. It is a visually stunning reward without needing to summit the mountain.
*   **The Cascades:** Medium to high reward for medium effort. Parts of these can sometimes be spotted briefly from the highway if you know exactly where to look!
*   **Hidden Falls:** Medium to high effort with a slightly lower reward. It is a beautiful waterfall, but reaching it requires a bit of a standard creek-hopping slog.

## Reaching High Camp and the Summit

For the adventurous, the walk to the Terraces is roughly a **12-kilometre return trip** and is strictly classified as a **Grade 5 hike**. You will need excellent shoes, great balance for rock hopping, and the upper-body strength to hoist yourself over large boulders.

If you keep pushing past the lower creek features, you will eventually hit an area known as **High Camp**. Marked by a massive monolithic boulder, High Camp serves as a spectacular vantage point offering views out across Chunda Bay and Cape Cleveland. It is the perfect spot to camp, catch a sunrise, and take a breather before making a push for the true summit of Mt Elliott, which towers at over 1,000 metres high.

> **The Summit Route:** Pushing to the summit from High Camp is a serious undertaking. Hikers follow the creek until hitting an impenetrable waterfall, at which point you must scramble steeply up onto the ridgeline to follow it to the top.

## The Golden Rule: Respecting Private Property

Why is this area still so pristine and well-preserved? It comes down to access. The entry point to this section of the national park lies entirely on private property owned by a local farmer.

Because of past instances where unprepared or disrespectful visitors have caused issues, getting permission independently can be incredibly difficult. It requires extensive research, building trust, and knowing who to contact. 

This is where walking with the **Townsville Bushwalking Club** makes all the difference. Over the last 62 years, the club has carefully nurtured relationships with local landholders to ensure sustainable, respectful, and permitted access to these beautiful locations.
