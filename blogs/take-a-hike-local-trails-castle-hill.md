Welcome back to the Take A Hike podcast blog! In our latest episode, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to dive into the incredible trail networks right here in our own backyard. You don't always have to travel out to Cape Bowling Green Bay or up to Paluma to find an adventure... sometimes the best tracks are staring you right in the face.

## Recent Adventures: Hidden Gorges and Paluma Cake

Before diving into our local city peaks, the team caught up on their recent trail highlights. 

Cherry and the club recently headed out to **Hidden Gorges**, exploring various creek systems and range drop-offs. Apparently, there is even a "mini Uluru" formation tucked away out there! 

Meanwhile, Luen took a more laid-back, three-hour "excursion" to **Forgotten Falls** in Paluma. Walking along the old logging road known as the K Traverse, her group made it out to the spectacular falls and celebrated a friend's birthday with cake right by Platypus Pool.

## Castle Hill: More Than Just the Goat Track

Did you know that up until 1864, Townsville was actually called **Castle Town**, named after our iconic pink granite monolith? It was renamed Townsville in 1865, but Castle Hill remains the heart of the city. Historically, the hill suffered from illegal logging and a massive stray goat problem (hence the name of the famous Goat Track!) before being formally gazetted as a recreational reserve in 1888.

Today, Castle Hill boasts over 20 tracks, plus a thriving rock climbing and bouldering scene (complete with its own dedicated local guidebook). While everyone knows the main routes, Cherry and Luen shared some of their favorite lesser-known trails and hidden gems:

*   **Dianella Track:** Starting from the Western Cemetery, this is a club favorite. It features a steep climb up a big rock to the road, but also connects to great offshoots.
*   **Triandra Track:** Branching off to the left from Dianella, this track skirts the base of the old quarry. If you have a bird's-eye view, you can still see a massive heart sign laid out near the quarry from an old marriage proposal!
*   **The Scramble Track:** A surprising, steep little scramble that links Triandra to the Ironbark Track and leads you straight up to the tip of the Summit Track.
*   **Widowmaker:** Luen's top pick for its sheer exposure, amazing breezes, and great Instagram-worthy shots on the pink granite slabs. It starts where the stairs, Goat Track, and Cutheringa Track meet at the wooden platform.

### The Great Stairs Debate

They also discussed the stairs that were installed on tracks like Dianella a couple of years ago. While some trail runners groan because it impacts their downhill times, the team agrees the infrastructure is a massive win for environmental conservation, effectively stopping the severe erosion that was tearing up the landscape.

Speaking of trail running, Blair tried to coax out Luen's fastest time up Castle Hill. Back in his peak training days, Luen could smash out the Cutheringa Track in an incredible **10 to 12 minutes**! Don't worry if you take 25 minutes or more... Cherry admits she prefers stopping for chips!

## Other Urban Tracks: Mount Louisa & Douglas Hills

If you want to escape the Castle Hill crowds, Townsville has plenty of other fantastic fitness and bushwalking networks:

### Mount Louisa
Thanks to recent council developments, Mount Louisa has proper, formed trails that help break up the fitness-walker traffic. The most popular track starts at the corner of Western Street and Bayswater Road. From the summit, you can connect to the **Crest Trail** towards the radar or explore the network heading down toward Dalrymple Road (just watch out for long grass depending on the season!).

### Douglas Hills
While primarily a mountain bike reserve, Douglas Hills has dedicated walking tracks. You can hike up from the streetscape of Windarra Avenue or tackle the **JCU Stairs** to reach the JCU Summit, which then links directly to the main Douglas Hills Summit.
