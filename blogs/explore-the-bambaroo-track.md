In the latest episode of the *Take a Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke to dive deep into one of North Queensland's lesser-known gems: **The Bambaroo Track**.

While many hikers head straight to the top of Paluma, the Bambaroo Track kicks off right at the bottom of the range. Located about an hour north of Townsville (just past the iconic Frosty Mangoes and Eleanor Creek), this trail offers immediate rewards, rich history, and stunning waterfall scrambles.

---

## Starting Out: The Launchpad

Because the track begins on private property, you will need to grab permission before setting off (check out **wanderstories.space** for the details). 

The initial stretch is a steep, 40-minute trudge for the average hiker. However, your efforts are quickly rewarded when you reach a flat landing featuring an Optus/Telstra tower. This spot serves as a launchpad for hang gliders, who catch the thermals here to fly all the way to Wallaman! It also boasts incredible panoramic views stretching across the landscape and out to the ocean.

## Giant Figs and Hidden Falls

Once past the tower, the vegetation shifts dramatically from guinea grass and lantana into a dense, beautiful mape and rainforest canopy. 

*   **The Giant Fig Trees:** You don't have to travel all the way to the Atherton Tablelands to see massive, curtain-like fig trees. A short detour at the trail junction leads to spectacular, accessible giants right in Townsville's backyard.
*   **Insulator Creek:** Continuing along, you will drop into the Valley of Insulator. Keep an eye out for historic tree blazes featuring carved initials from the early days of the track.
*   **Fig Tree Falls:** Located about 100 meters upstream from the creek intersection, this stunning waterfall literally flows right out through a fig tree. With recent consistent rainfall, it is flowing perfectly.
*   **Insulator Creek Falls:** Head 100 to 200 meters downstream to find a dramatic gorge. Getting down requires a bit of a "crab crawl" on your bottom, so take extra care on the slippery, wet rocks!

## Climbing to the Top

For those looking for a challenge, the Bambaroo Track continues steeply up the ridgeline, passing old mining properties where you can spot rusted spanners and historic bottle dumps. Remember to look and take photos, but leave the artifacts exactly as you found them.

Eventually, the track tops out near **Gardes' Lookout** (named after a local miner) and **Shays Clearing** at the top of the Paluma Range. 

> **Bambaroo vs. Bullocky Toms:** How does it compare to other range climbs? Luen notes that while Bullocky Toms and Foxlee's tracks have their own incredible rewards, Bambaroo is slightly less steep than Bullocky Toms and gives you its best rewards-like the fig trees and falls-much earlier in the hike. Because it is less frequented, it offers a true wilderness experience.

## Practice for Overnight Hikes

With a 1,000-meter elevation gain, the Townsville Bushwalking Club is currently looking at utilizing the Bambaroo Track as a perfect training ground for members wanting to practice overnight hiking. It is an ideal, on-track challenge for testing your fitness while carrying a full overnight pack.

### Practical Tips for the Trail:
*   **Check the Overgrowth:** If the track hasn't been cleared recently, the lawyer vine and vegetation can get thick.
*   **Safety First:** Scrambling near the gorges can be dangerous after heavy rain. Watch your footing.
*   **Get Involved:** Want to join an organized walk? Head over to **townsvillebushwalkingclub.com** to check out upcoming weekend trips and club events.

Whether you are heading out to tackle the full range or just packing a picnic to see the giant fig trees, the Bambaroo Track is a must-do. Just don't forget to stop for an ice cream at Frosty Mangoes on the way home!
