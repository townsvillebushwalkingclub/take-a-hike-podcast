## The Great Gore-Tex Hiking Shoe Debate

Blair kicks off this episode with a controversial take: he absolutely hates Gore-Tex (GTX) hiking shoes. While they are marketed as breathable and waterproof, Blair argues that once water inevitably gets over your ankle during a North Queensland creek crossing, it stays trapped inside. 

Luen Warneke and Cherry Judge weigh in with different perspectives. Cherry values Gore-Tex boots for their toughness and excellent grip on slippery rocks, though she admits they run hot in the tropical summer. Luen takes a pragmatic approach, focusing on fit and affordability, recommending brands like Salomon or Decathlon's Quechua line. 

**Practical Boot Tips from the Team:**

* **Lifespan:** Expect to get around 500 to 800 kilometres (about 12 months of heavy use) out of a good pair of hiking shoes before the stitching and soles wear out.
* **The Tropical Factor:** Waterproof linings can trap water inside just as effectively as they keep it out. Consider lightweight, quick-drying non-GTX mesh shoes if you do a lot of creek walking.

## Exploring Paluma's Historic K-Traverse

The main adventure of the day takes us up to Paluma to tackle the **K-Traverse**, a challenging 14 to 15-kilometre trail that takes between five and seven hours to complete. 

Following a network of old logging roads used between the 1930s and 1970s, the K-Traverse is packed with fascinating historical relics.

### Key Highlights and Trail Notes

* **The Start:** Look for the old rusty gate about 1.5 kilometres along the Paluma Dam Road. Just 20 metres in, you will spot a massive, ancient tree that miraculously escaped the loggers.
* **Historical Relics:** A few hundred metres into the track, there is an old boulder dump filled with historic glass bottles and vials. Further along, you will even pass an old sawmill site. Remember to leave these artifacts in place for others to admire!
* **Water Wonders:** The trail offers access to beautiful water spots, including the popular detour to Forgotten Falls and Williams Creek (home to Platypus Lagoon), as well as the more off-track Echo Creek and Big Birthday Creek.

> **Hiker Note:** The K-Traverse requires solid navigation skills. Beyond the well-maintained section leading to Forgotten Falls, the trail is heavily overgrown. You will need a bit of imagination to follow the old logging road, so be sure to bring your secateurs!
