## Catching Waterfalls Post-Rain

If you have been looking at your muddy backyard lately wondering when the rain will give it a rest, the *Take a Hike* team has some excellent news. While hiking in a heavy downpour isn't always ideal, the absolute best time to discover North Queensland's hidden gems is right after a heavy rain shower. 

Host Blair Woodcock sat down with Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke from the Townsville Bushwalking Club to chat about the incredible seasonal creeks and secret waterfalls that burst to life across Magnetic Island and the mainland after a solid drenching.

---

## Magnetic Island's Hidden Gems

Magnetic Island (or "Maggie" to the locals) is currently pumping, with almost every creek transforming into a scenic water feature. Here are the top seasonal spots to check out:

### Endeavour Falls (Arcadia)
Located at the back of Arcadia at the end of Endeavour Road, this is the island's most accessible waterfall. It is a quick five-minute walk from the road, making it incredibly popular. 

* **Safety Note:** The waterfall features an upper and lower section. The lower section is currently **closed** due to recent landslides and wet weather injuries. However, the top section remains open and features a lovely little swimming pool. Just be mindful of falling rocks, as a massive boulder reshaped this area back in 2013!

### Dog Shed Waterhole / Cockle Bay
Heading out towards West Point, right where the road turns to dirt, you will cross Cockle Creek. If you follow an old track in for about 50 metres past an abandoned, graffiti-covered concrete structure (hence the local name "Dog Shed"), you will find a great seasonal flow. This spot acts as a shallow toddler pool, but remember: no diving!

### Huntingfield Falls
For the ultimate Instagram-worthy shot, Huntingfield Falls on the north side of the island is running heavy right now. While it is a very grueling off-track walk to get there by foot, the easiest and most spectacular way to visit is by taking a boat or kayaking around the headland from Horseshoe Bay or West Point.

## Mainland Waterfalls Worth the Drive

If you are staying on the mainland, you don't have to travel far to see the water flow. 

* **Bridal Falls (Hervey's Range):** You can drive right past this beautiful waterfall. There is a safe vehicle pull-over area on the right-hand side of the road, and you will almost always spot a photographer or two with tripods capturing the perfect shot.
* **Paluma Range:** The drive up the range is stunning right now, with seasonal creeks popping out along the roadside at every turn.

---

## Waterfall Safety First

While chasing waterfalls is exciting, the team stresses the importance of safety:
* Never jump or dive into natural rock pools unless you have thoroughly tested the depth with goggles.
* Be aware of submerged hazards like upward-pointing logs or rocks shifted by recent torrents.
* Stay clear of unstable edges and landslide-prone zones.
