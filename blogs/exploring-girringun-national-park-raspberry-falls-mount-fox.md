## Take a Hike to Girringun National Park

In this episode of the Take A Hike podcast, hosts Blair Woodcock, Luen Warneke, and Cherry Judge set their sights on the vast and rugged terrain of **Girringun National Park**. If you are looking for a weekend escape north of Ingham, this is a part of the world that has so many places to explore.

### Discovering Raspberry Falls

A highlight of the conversation is **Raspberry Falls**. This stunning, terraced waterfall is a favorite for those who enjoy a rewarding, off-the-beaten-path experience. 

* **The Experience:** Unlike many falls where you view the face, at Raspberry Falls, you walk through the bush and emerge at the top of the waterfall, looking out over the landscape all the way to the ocean.
* **The Trail:** It is approximately a 5km return walk, taking about 1 to 3 hours depending on your pace. The team classifies this as a Grade 4 walk because the trail is not well-formed. Navigation skills are essential, and walkers are encouraged to follow the creek.
* **Practical Tip:** Before you go, head to [Wanderstories](https://wanderstories.space) to download the map. It is the best resource for ensuring you stay on track.
* **Enjoy the View:** Once you arrive, there are plenty of beautiful pools for swimming and ideal spots for a picnic.

### Pair Your Trip with Mount Fox

Since it is a bit of a drive from Townsville, the team suggests making the most of your trip by combining your hike with a visit to **Mount Fox**. 

Mount Fox is an ancient volcanic crater and a sight to behold. While it is a steep hike to reach the rim, it is shorter than Townsville's own Castle Hill. The view looking down into the crater is spectacular, and you might even spot local kangaroos grazing near the top. 

If you want to stay overnight, there is an official National Parks campground located at the base of the hill.

### Pro Tips for Your Adventure

* **Access:** You can reach this area via Ingham or through the scenic Hidden Valley route. 
* **Make a Weekend of It:** For a longer trip, consider adding a stop at **Broadwater State Park** to float down the river-a perfect way to relax after some time on the trails.
* **Stay Safe:** Always carry plenty of water, and ensure you are prepared for the conditions.
