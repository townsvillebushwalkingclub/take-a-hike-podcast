## Chasing Untouched Waterfalls in the Misty Mountains

If you are feeling a little back-to-work blues and already counting down the weeks until the next long weekend, we have the perfect adventure to help you plan your next escape. On the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sat down with Townsville Bushwalking Club regulars Luen Warneke and Cherry Judge to chat about a hidden gem that will get you well away from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Located up north in Wooroonooran National Park - near the Innisfail and Atherton Tablelands area - lies a stunning, untouched paradise within the Misty Mountains. If you are looking for spectacular waterfalls that you won't find on standard trail apps like AllTrails, this is the trek for you.

---

## The Journey to Cowley Falls

While there is an old, unmaintained National Park track that leads to the top of Cowley Falls in about 30 to 60 minutes from the Palmerston Highway, the real adventure lies in heading to the base. 

Here is how Luen tackles this off-the-beaten-path route:

*   **The Start:** Begin at Gravel Pit Road just off the side of the highway where the rainforest starts, near the local tea plantation and private property.
*   **Friendship Falls:** Hike through the lush rainforest following a pink-taped track down the range. This track was originally used by kayakers and paddlers to access the South Johnston River. It leads you directly to Friendship Falls (also known as Little Cowley Falls), which looks like a miniature version of the main event.
*   **The River Walk:** From Friendship Falls, walk about a kilometre upstream along the South Johnston River to Mitcha Creek, then head a couple of hundred metres further upstream to the base of Cowley Falls.

> **A Word of Warning from the Crew:** Yes, there are actually crocodiles in this part of the Misty Mountains! Paddlers and rafters frequently spot estuarine (saltwater) crocodiles in the South Johnston River. To stay safe, the team advises walking upstream rather than swimming in the main river. Once you get past the massive boulders and rocks near Mitcha Creek, it is highly unlikely crocs can navigate the terrain, making the base of the falls much safer.

## What Makes Cowley Falls So Special?

Standing at roughly 80 metres high, Cowley Falls is a jaw-dropping sight. It is a near-single drop where the water runs dramatically down the side of the cliff face into a super lush, untouched amphitheatre. 

Because so few people visit this remote spot, the surrounding environment is pristine, covered in green rainforest and beautiful wild orchids. 

Even better? There is a cave directly underneath the waterfall. You can actually crawl around behind the curtain of water and peer out through the falls. There is also a second cave on the side of the cliff that offers the absolute perfect vantage point for photos.

### The Return Challenge
For the average hiker, this return journey will take about **4 to 6 hours**.

You can easily return the way you came, but if you are up for a physical challenge, there is a very steep ridge line right beside Cowley Falls. It requires scrambling on all fours, pulling yourself up through the mud by grabbing onto roots and vegetation. Once you reach the top, you contour around the cliff face to the top of the falls and catch the old, overgrown National Park track back out to the highway.

## More Secret Spots Near Henrietta Camping Area

If you want to turn this into a weekend trip, the **Henrietta Camping Area** is the perfect base camp. It is only about a five-minute drive from the start of the Cowley Falls route, and it positions you perfectly for other nearby adventures.

If you are staying in the area, make sure to check out:
*   **Nandroya Falls:** A beautiful and well-known waterfall walk easily accessed from the campground.
*   **Tipun Falls:** Another insider secret! Located just up the road from the camping area in the opposite direction, follow a creek about 100 metres into the rainforest to find this small, untouched, and completely unofficial waterfall.
*   **The Atherton Tablelands:** Only a few kilometres away, making it the perfect post-hike stop to grab a famous local milkshake from the dairy.

Detailed information and coordinates for these hidden trails can be found on Wanderstories.
