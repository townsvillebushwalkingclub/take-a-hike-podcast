## Hidden Wonders North of Ingham

In the latest episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast, host Blair Woodcock sits down with Cherry Judge and Luen Warneke from the Townsville Bushwalking Club to chat about the massive, waterfall-rich wonderland that is **Girringun National Park**. 

While many locals know the park for Wallaman Falls or Blencoe Falls, the team shines a spotlight on a lesser-known gem: **Raspberry Falls**, along with some fantastic ways to turn a trip up north into a full weekend adventure.

---

## The Terraced Beauty of Raspberry Falls

Raspberry Falls is an incredible terraced waterfall where the trail leads you right onto the top of the falls rather than at the base. You are surrounded by cascading water with stunning, open views stretching all the way out to the ocean.

Here is what you need to know before you go:

* **The Track:** It is a **Grade 4** walk because the trail is not well-formed. It follows an old road track and requires navigation skills.
* **Distance & Time:** It is a **5-kilometre return** trip. It takes anywhere from 1 to 3 hours depending on your pace, but you will want to budget extra time to relax at the top.
* **Highlights:** There are plenty of little rock pools perfect for swimming and lovely flat areas to enjoy a picnic.

> **Navigator Note:** Because the trail is unformed, make sure to download a map from **wanderstories.space** or carry a topographic map before heading out. Keep close to the creek and you will find your way.

---

## Make it a Day Trip: Mount Fox and Beyond

Since Girringun is a long drive from Townsville, the crew recommends pairing Raspberry Falls with a visit to **Mount Fox**, an ancient volcanic crater sitting just on the other side of the road.

Mount Fox is shorter than Castle Hill, but it features a steep, rudimentary track leading up to the crater rim. Standing on the edge and looking inside the crater is a spectacular sight, and you can often spot kangaroos grazing along the top. 

### Where to Camp and Explore
If you want to stretch your trip into a multi-day getaway, you have plenty of options:

* **Mount Fox Base:** There is an official National Parks campground at the bottom of the hill where you can set up camp for the night.
* **Broadwater State Park:** A short drive away, this spot is perfect for camping and floating down the creek on inflatable tire tubes.
* **The Scenic Route:** You can access the area via Ingham or take the adventurous route back through **Hidden Valley**, turning past the Gregory Development Road to connect toward Harvey Range or Paluma.
