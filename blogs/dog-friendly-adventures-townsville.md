## Paws on the Trail: Hiking with Your Best Friend

If you love hitting the trails but hate leaving your four-legged companion at home, this week's episode of the *Take A Hike* podcast is for you. Hosts Blair Woodcock and Cherry Judge are both passionate dog owners dealing with the unique challenges of hiking with pups. On the other hand, Luen Warneke represents the non-pet owners (at least until her new cat, Tia, starts lead training!). 

Whether you have a high-energy working breed like Cherry's one-year-old Border Collie Kelpie cross, Vlad, or lazier companions like Blair's two golden retrievers, finding the right track is key. 

Here is a comprehensive wrap-up of the best dog-friendly adventures and walking tracks around Townsville discussed on the show.

---

## Local Favourites & Easy Tracks

### Castle Hill & Mount Louisa
For a quick and reliable outing, you can't go past Townsville's classic landmarks. Both Castle Hill and Mount Louisa offer excellent options, provided your dogs are kept on a leash. 

*   **Blair's Tip:** Avoid the steep steps if your dogs aren't up for it. Instead, stick to the old fire trails and dirt roads (like the Dalrymple side of Mount Louisa) for a gentler incline.

### Ross River Circuit & Ross River Dam Wall
If flat ground is more your style, the Ross River Circuit bike path is an easy, paved option. Cherry also highly recommends walking along the Ross River Dam wall. It offers a flat, decent-length walk where you can easily do an hour or more in one direction before turning back.

### Douglas Mountain Bike Reserve
Looking for something a bit more rugged close to town? There is a proper walking track up the Douglas Mountain Bike Reserve. While the JCU side features quite a few stairs, it transitions into a great dirt track that high-energy dogs love.

---

## Scenic Escapes & Beaches

### Secret Beach & The Northern Beaches
For a coastal walk, head to Secret Beach in South Townsville, located right at the opening of the creek estuary. Further north, Saunders Beach to Toolakea offers fantastic stretches of sand. 

> **A Note on Pallarenda:** While there is a designated pet-friendly space on the beach around Pallarenda (between entrances 9 and 10), the team notes it can be a bit of a "luck of the draw" with aggressive dogs. Always stay vigilant!

### Jensen Hills & Mount Bohle
Near the residential developments out past Jensen, you can find brilliant, interlinking dirt tracks. Try parking at the end of Apple Tree Drive (being careful not to block any driveways) to explore the hills out the back. 

---

## Epic Day Walks & Weekend Getaways

### Black River Adventure
During the dry season, the sandy bed of the Black River opens up a massive opportunity for a proper adventure. A great route is walking bridge-to-bridge from the Black River Road Bridge down to the Bruce Highway Bridge.

For an even longer challenge (roughly 20+ kilometres), you can park at the recreational reserve at the base of Hervey's Range near the old hotel. From there, walk into Palm Tree Creek and follow it through to the Alice or Black River.

### Elliott Springs & Mount Margaret
*   **Bindle Ridge (Elliott Springs):** A fantastic pet-friendly option, though it does feature a few steps.
*   **Mount Margaret (Alice River):** A brilliant workout on a pure dirt track the entire way up.

### Overnighters: Charters Towers & Running River
If you want a weekend escape, check out **Big Bend** and **Flat Rock** near Charters Towers, which are council-managed areas perfect for a getaway. Alternatively, check out **Zigzag Station** near Running River. It is a newly opened private property where you can pay for a campsite and bring your dogs along.

## The Golden Rules of Dog Hiking

While exploring these gorgeous spots, the team emphasized a few non-negotiable rules to keep our tracks open and safe for everyone:

*   **Keep them on a lead:** This protects your dog, other walkers, and the local wildlife.
*   **No National Parks:** Remember, domestic pets are strictly prohibited in gazetted National Parks.
*   **Pick up after your dog:** As Blair passionately stressed, always bag your dog's waste and pack it out with you. Leave the trail pristine!

For more detailed maps and a complete database of local pet-friendly tracks, look up the dog-friendly walks on **wanderstories.space**.

Happy hiking!
